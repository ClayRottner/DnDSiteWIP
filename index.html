D&D 5th Edition Character Creator

The Basics

Making a character for Dungeons and Dragons 5th Edition is just a handful of steps... The problem is that for each question, there's about a dozen choices. The point of this page is to help smooth out character creation for you so that you're ready to go and have fun adventuring.

We will be going through the steps needed for a character one by one, and explaining each option along with the pros and cons.

The steps of character creation we will go through are as follows, and in this order:

  1. Choose a race
  2. Choose a class
  3. Choose a background
  4. Choose equipment
  5. Assign ability scores
  6. Assign proficiencies

Choosing your ability scores

Here we will choose how good or bad your character is in the 6 abilitie categories. Strength, Dexterity, Intelligence, Wisdom, Constitution, and Charisma.


There are generally two ways of getting the points to allocate into the abilities:

  • Use a predefined set of stats (15, 14, 13, 12, 10, 8), distributing them as you like.

  • Roll 4d6 and remove the lowest, adding the remaining 3 dice total to create a stat. Repeat 5 more times.

Please choose which way you would like to do it, and fill in each ability.
